About PerAgents

PerAgents focuses on the critical systems-level challenges of transitioning from narrow, single-task edge AI to general-purpose, autonomous AI agents operating in physical environments. Currently, foundation models are trapped in the cloud due to massive computational requirements. This workshop explores how large-scale models such as Large Language Models (LLMs), Vision-Language Models (VLMs), and Multimodal Language Models (MLMs) can be compressed, decentralized, and securely integrated with pervasive sensor networks.

A defining feature of pervasive agentic systems is their domain-agnostic architectures. The exact same edge-computing architecture, split learning pipeline, and VLM integration used to fuse IoT telemetry and satellite data for a real-time decision-support system in precision agriculture can be deployed in a clinical setting to fuse wearable sensor streams for continuous patient monitoring. PerAgents investigates the underlying system architecture that remains constant even as the environmental context changes.

Call for Papers

This workshop will focus on, but not be limited to, the following systems-level topics:

Systems-Level Technical Topics

  • Hardware-software co-design for edge agents
  • Decentralized and split/federated agentic learning
  • Multimodal sensor fusion for VLMs/MLMs
  • Trust, safety, and local overrides for physical actuation
  • Bandwidth-efficient distributed inference
  • Cross-domain agent adaptation algorithms
  • Agentic synchronization across IoT mesh networks

Target Community

A primary goal of PerAgents is to bring new interdisciplinary researchers into the PerCom ecosystem who might not traditionally attend a pervasive computing systems conference. We specifically aim to attract and intersect three distinct groups:
  • The AI & Foundation Model Community: Researchers seeking hardware-software deployment solutions for LLMs and VLMs outside of centralized data centers.
  • Industry Practitioners & Applied Domain Experts: Engineers and architects from AgriTech, Digital Health, and Smart Cities who are actively attempting to deploy autonomous agents in the physical field.
  • The Core Systems & Edge Engineering Community: Traditional PerCom researchers looking to transition from narrow, single-task ML classifiers to generalized, multi-agent frameworks.
